Round Eyes
Apply lighter eyeshadow from your lash linen to the brow bone. Apply the darker shade on your eye crease, creating a "V" shape.
Apply eyeliner starting a quarter way from your inner corner.
Try to stick as close to the lash line as possible. Don't be afraid of creating a cat eye or winged eyeliner.
Droopy Eyes
Try not to apply mascara to your bottom lashes. Curl the upper lashes and focus on those instead.
Try to keep eyeliner to the upperlid only.
Avoid shimmery shadows, since they will create the image of droopier eyes.
Stick to a few basic shadows that will even out your lids.
Hooded Eyes
Avoid applying dark shadows over the lid as this will create a "tired" look.
Avoid highlighting the brow bone too much, as this will shorten the eyelid space.
If you line the bottom lash line, don't forget to line the top as well.
You can though, line the top lash without lining the bottom.

Monolid Eyes
Skip the wing when applying eyeliner, instead, create one by blending dark shadows upwards and at an angle.
An ombree effect for eye shadows creates a seemingly open eye look.
